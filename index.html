Web Data Project

The Web Data Project is a partnership between leading researchers in the social sciences to collect and organize relevant web activity into research-quality datasets. Access to datasets is free for all qualified research institutions and WDP provides a hosted service for regular, reliable, and polite web crawling.

Many researchers are now crawling web pages as a means of data collection. Many websites, on the other hand, actively prevent unauthorized crawlers in order to protect both their users' privacy and their company's competitive advantage. Rogue web scraping might serve a researcher in their current agenda, but at the cost of jeopardizing user privacy or risking their ability to gather data longitudinally. WDP seeks to provide researchers, today and tomorrow, with invaluable empirical evidence while still respecting the discretion of content owners and the privacy of content creators.

If you are a researcher that is interested in creating a new dataset, you only need to contribute code for crawling the desired sites and generating the dataset. After contacting the website owners, the crawler will then be run regularly on WDP infrastructure and the dataset made available to all other researchers in member institutions.
